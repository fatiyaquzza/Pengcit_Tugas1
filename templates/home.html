Histogram Equalization

Histogram equalization is a image processing technique used to enhance the contrast and dynamic range of an image by redistributing the pixel intensities in a way that maximizes the utilization of the available intensity levels. It works by transforming the histogram of an image so that the resulting histogram is approximately uniform, spreading out the pixel values over the entire intensity range. This improves the visibility of details in both dark and bright regions of the image, making it particularly useful for enhancing images with uneven lighting or low contrast.
